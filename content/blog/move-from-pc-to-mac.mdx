The scariest part of switching is usually not the Mac itself. It is the thought of moving years of files, email, photos, and bookmarks off your old PC and somehow rebuilding all of it on a machine you barely know.

You do not have to. Apple makes a free tool called Migration Assistant that does the move for you, in one pass, and drops everything into the right apps on the Mac.

## What it actually moves

Migration Assistant copies your stuff from a Windows PC and puts each thing where it belongs on the Mac:

- Email and email accounts from Outlook or Windows Mail land in the Mail app.
- Contacts and calendar appointments land in Contacts and Calendar.
- Your music, photos, and documents come across to the Music app and your Home folder.
- Bookmarks from Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and the rest move into Safari.

It does not erase your PC and it does not change macOS. It only copies, so your old machine stays exactly as it was.

## How it works, in plain terms

Both computers go on the same Wi-Fi network. You install the free Windows Migration Assistant on the PC, open Migration Assistant on the Mac, match a code that appears on screen, choose what you want to bring over, and let it run. Large transfers can take a while, so it is the kind of thing you start and walk away from.

Two things are worth doing first: update both Windows and macOS, and turn off any antivirus, firewall, or VPN on both machines during the transfer, since those can interrupt it. One more detail that trips people up: download the version of Windows Migration Assistant that matches your Mac's macOS version. Apple lists the right one for each.

For the exact steps, the current download links, and the full list of what gets transferred, follow Apple's official guide: [Transfer from PC to Mac with Migration Assistant](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102565).

## You are not starting from scratch

This is the part that calms most switchers down. Your files are not trapped on the old machine, and you are not retyping your life into a new one. You point the Mac at your PC, pick what to bring, and your documents, photos, and accounts are waiting for you on the other side. The switch is a move, not a fresh start.
